Mining Project Timelines: From Discovery to Production

By Margaret Olsen · 2026-03-23

mining timelinedevelopment stagesfeasibilitypermitting

Developing a mining project from initial discovery to commercial production is a long and complex process. For investors, understanding the timeline and the key milestones along the way is essential for evaluating development-stage companies like GRML.

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

The Mining Development Stages

Stage 1: Exploration (Years 1-5)

The exploration phase involves identifying and assessing a mineral deposit:

  • Reconnaissance: Initial geological mapping, geophysical surveys, and geochemical sampling. Cost: US$1-5M. Timeline: 1-2 years
  • Target generation: Identifying specific drill targets based on exploration data. Cost: US$2-10M. Timeline: 1-2 years
  • Drilling: Diamond drilling to test targets and define mineralization. Cost: US$5-20M. Timeline: 1-3 years
  • Resource definition: Sufficient drilling to estimate a mineral resource (Inferred, Indicated, or Measured). Cost: US$10-30M. Timeline: 1-2 years

Milestone: Publication of an initial mineral resource estimate (NI 43-101 or equivalent).

Stage 2: Preliminary Economic Assessment (PEA) (Year 3-5)

A preliminary assessment of the project's economic potential:

  • Scope order-of-magnitude study based on the resource estimate and preliminary cost estimates
  • Accuracy range: +50% to -30% on capital costs
  • Purpose: Screen projects for further investment. A positive PEA justifies proceeding to pre-feasibility
  • Cost: US$1-5M
  • Timeline: 6-12 months

Note: A PEA is preliminary in nature and includes inferred mineral resources that are considered too speculative geologically to have economic considerations applied.

Stage 3: Pre-Feasibility Study (PFS) (Year 5-7)

A more detailed assessment of project economics:

  • Detailed drilling and metallurgical testing
  • Preliminary mine design and processing plant design
  • Environmental baseline studies initiated
  • Capital and operating cost estimates with greater accuracy
  • Accuracy range: +30% to -15% on capital costs
  • Purpose: Justify the significant investment required for a full feasibility study and EIA
  • Cost: US$10-30M
  • Timeline: 12-18 months

Milestone: Positive pre-feasibility study justifies proceeding to full feasibility and EIA.

Stage 4: Feasibility Study (FS) (Year 7-9)

The definitive assessment of project economics and technical viability:

  • Comprehensive drilling and resource upgrade (Indicated to Measured)
  • Definitive mine plan and processing plant design
  • Detailed capital and operating cost estimates
  • Offtake agreements and financing term sheets
  • Accuracy range: +15% to -10% on capital costs
  • Purpose: Support a production decision (go/no-go) and financing
  • Cost: US$20-50M
  • Timeline: 18-24 months

Milestone: Positive feasibility study supports production decision and project financing.

Stage 5: Environmental Impact Assessment and Permitting (Year 5-9, overlapping with PFS/FS)

The environmental and regulatory approval process:

  • Environmental baseline studies: 2-3 years of data collection
  • EIA report: Comprehensive environmental impact assessment
  • Public consultation: Mandatory community engagement
  • Permit review: Government review and approval process
  • Timeline: 2-5 years (can overlap with PFS and FS)
  • Cost: US$20-50M

Stage 6: Financing (Year 9-10)

Securing capital for mine construction:

  • Equity financing: Issuing shares to raise development capital
  • Debt financing: Bank loans or bond issuance (project finance)
  • Royalty/stream: Upfront payment for future production
  • Timeline: 6-12 months

Stage 7: Construction (Year 10-13)

Building the mine:

  • Mine site preparation: Roads, camp, power, water
  • Processing plant construction: Crushing, grinding, flotation, tailings
  • Infrastructure: Port, airstrip, utilities
  • Timeline: 2-4 years for a major Arctic mine
  • Cost: US$600M-1.25B for Skaergaard

Stage 8: Commissioning and Production (Year 13-14)

Testing and ramping up operations:

  • Commissioning: Testing individual systems and the integrated plant
  • Ramp-up: Gradually increasing production to design capacity over 6-18 months
  • Commercial production declaration: When the mine achieves sustained production at design capacity

Total Timeline

For a greenfield Arctic mining project like Skaergaard:

  • Exploration to resource: 3-5 years
  • PEA to PFS: 2-3 years
  • Feasibility + EIA + permitting: 3-5 years
  • Financing: 1 year
  • Construction: 2-4 years
  • Commissioning: 1 year

Total: 12-18 years from exploration to commercial production

Value Creation Milestones

For investors, the key value creation milestones are:

1. Initial resource publication: First resource estimate validates the geological thesis
2. Positive PEA: Preliminary economics confirm the project could be economic
3. Resource upgrade: Infill drilling upgrades resource to higher confidence categories
4. Positive PFS: More detailed economics support the investment case
5. EIA approval: Regulatory risk is reduced
6. Positive FS: Definitive economics support financing
7. Financing secured: Capital is in hand for construction
8. Construction commencement: Physical progress reduces execution risk
9. Commercial production: Cash flow begins, de-risking the investment

Timeline Risk

Exploration License Risk: GRML holds three Mineral Exploration Licenses (MELs) totaling 877 km². MEL 2012-25 (Sødalen camp and airstrip, 16 km²) and MEL 2021-10 (754 km² exploration area) both expire December 31, 2026. MEL 2007-01 (107 km², hosts the Skaergaard Intrusion) remains active until December 31, 2027. These renewals represent a material near-term risk factor.

Mining project timelines are often delayed:

  • Typical delays: 1-3 years beyond planned timeline
  • Common causes: Permitting delays, financing delays, construction challenges, commodity price cycles, political changes
  • For Arctic projects: Delays may be longer due to short field seasons, weather disruptions, and first-in-jurisdiction complexity

Conclusion

Developing a greenfield Arctic mining project from exploration to production typically takes 12-18 years. The timeline is driven by the sequential nature of exploration, feasibility, EIA, permitting, financing, and construction. For investors in development-stage companies like GRML, understanding the timeline and key value creation milestones is essential for evaluating the investment thesis and managing expectations. Each milestone represents a de-risking event that can catalyze re-rating of the company's value.
